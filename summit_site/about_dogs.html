About Alaskan Dogs

Personality

  • High-Energy dogs, especially when they're puppies. They are trainable when provided with enough daily excercise. Aim for 40-80 minutes of excercise per day.
  • Friendly and good with children.
  • Sociable with other dogs.
  • Even-tempered and eager to please owners.
  • Protective of owners.
  • Prefers colder climates.
  • Intelligent and strong-willed minds which requires a confident owner.

Habits

  • Strong desire to chase and grab smaller animals such as cats.
  • Howls or destroys things when bored, which requires owners to stay attentive and keep the dog active
  • Attempts to escape kennels and yards.
  • Possiveness of food.
  • Can frequently dig and chew on items.

Other Interesting Facts

  • Alaskan dogs may have blue, brown, or black eyes.
  • They have the ability to run as fast as 30 mph.
  • Breeds such as samoyeds, huskys, and malamutes are some of the best sled dogs in the world.
  • Many Alaskan dog breeds have a strong pack instinct.
  • Barking frequency varies across each breed. Larger dogs such as Huskys or Malamutes may not bark as much as Samoyeds or Eskimos.
